Rainbow Six Siege Week 4 Recap

Taylor Thimmesh

A Star Emerges

This week our Siege team took to week 4 with a lot of momentum coming out of a nailbiting near come from behind series against Trinity Valley. Still currently without their first win, the team is making great strides and showing constant improvement. Facing down a dangerous Hutch CC team, JCC went into this match with the intent of showing they were no pushover themselves. The Jagaurs have yet to find their identity as a team, yet every week tends to show more and more potential. With some standout performance, flawless rounds, and a complete destruction in Game 2, JCC is poised to make a huge step into the National Spotlight in the coming months.

Game 1

Game 1 saw the Jags and Blue Dragons meeting on Clubhouse, a popular pick by JCC and one of their strongest maps. Coming into this game Danny "WhiteAssassin" Jones, the Team Captain for Rainbow, had made strict preparations for site control and contention. The Jags have struggled on the attacking side this season and looked to make a statement to both themselves and their opponent. Through the first two rounds, Hutch CC held strong on the defensive side going up 2-0 early. The Blue Dragons were led by Civvy, a very strong and aggressive player who hunts down his opponents with reckless abandon. The standout player from Hutch led his team to an early 5-1 lead reaching double digit kills within the first half. The final round saw Hutch player "Riles" clutch a 1v4, providing huge momentum going into their attacking side. JCC struggled to find a foothole, unable to even get into site. They were led by Noah at the halfway mark with 8 kills.



The Jags showed some life on the defensive side, winning the initial round to put the score at 2-5. Both teams had players step up in the proceeding rounds. The trio of "Civvy", "Earthly", and "Riles" combined for 30 kills going into the Match Point frame. A strong hold from Johnston pushed the game to another match point. With the win in sights, Hutch made this round count, closing out JCC and taking the first game 7-3. Whether this was the norm for those 3 mentioned Hutch players or a fluke, the Jaguars needed an answer in Game 2.

Game 2

Game 2 was played on Skyscraper, the map of choice for the Blue Dragons. This was not a very familiar map for the Jags, and is not often played in general among competitive settings. Something seemed to click for the Jaguars on this unfamiliar map. The team was playing together, trading out kills, and holding site strongly. Having chosen the defensive side first, JCC took a split half going into their attacking phase tied all at 3-3. Having struggled on the attacking side, this was a definitive moment as to whether this team can compete on both sides of the game. While the scoreboard would indicate that Noah "ConfirmedGuy" Netter was a man among boys, the entire team cam to play in the second half. Multiple rounds of each player getting kills to secure the round win. Chipper "Yeti" Starling, Jessica "Jess" Tovar, Cameron "GrandpaJoe" Langston, and Danny "WhiteAssassin" Jones all made plays to go along with Noah's 15-4 game. This appeared to be a breakout game for the newest member of the Rainbow Six roster. JCC took a strong Match Point lead at 6-3, eventually sealing the deal on a flawless performance winning the game 7-4. Hunting for their first win of the season, the Jags were poised to steal a seemingly out of reach series just two weeks ago.

Game 3

Game 3 came into everyones mind with high expectations. Beating Hutch on their map of choice gave a lot of momentum to the Jags going into the deciding match. This game would unfortunately not go in their favor. With the teams starring down an Oregon decider, a map the Jags are very familiar with, all of the wind was seemingly sucked out of their sails. The Jaguars came out flat as the Blue Dragons showed no sign of slowing down. Riles continued to dominate the game, leading Hutch to a very quick 6-0 lead going 10-3 into the second half. With their backs against the wall, the Jags managed to pull out a round to force a second match point. It was here, on Laundry room site, that the Bue Dragons slammed the door on the first potential win for JCC on the season. With so much potential shown, the question lingers on if this team will get over the hump. No doubt that the player skill is there, they simply need to put it together and finally show the true level at which they can play.

Player Interviews

Following the series, I took the time to reach out to the players for their thoughts about how the games went, what they can improve on, and how their mindeset is shifting heading into the final stretch of the season:

Danny "WhiteAssassin" Jones

Do you think Noah stood out this week in regards to his fragging ability, how does that factor into you guys ability to create plays?

I feel like Noah played excellent, and he has really stepped up for this being his first season. The team itself has made big improvements and I think that is attributed to just better teamwork. Playing off one another, playing trades, and our chemistry outside of the game has done a lot for us as a Team

Going into the next two weeks against very good teams, how do you feel like you guys stack up right now and how does that effect your playoff chances?

Going into the next two weeks, we got to get more practice in. Also, I feel like we need to mix it up a little bit to beat these next two teams. With the final three weeks we need to win out to have a chance at playoffs, we just need to take it one game at a time.

Noah "ConfirmedGuy" Netter

What was your mindset going into game 3 after your 15-4 performance on the enemy map choice?

My mindset during the second game was mainly focusing on Intel and the team call-outs. I believe Intel is key to Rainbow Six.

Do you feel as if the team looks to you for your ability to create space by fragging out?

I feel the team is now looking at me to clear some parts of the space. I can't do the fragging alone, so I rely on teammates for Intel of where enemies might be lurking.

Does the team, in your eyes, seem to be on an upward trajectory in terms of performance?

I do see our performance getting incredibly better each game. Each player is increasing their strategy of the game, which I find satisfying to see the improvement.

Chipper "Yeti" Starling

How are you seeing improvement throughout the team in the past two weeks considering you guys have made a huge jump in gameday performance?

We are becoming one of if not the best lock down defensive teams in the conference. I can tell off of how we dealt with Skyscraper tonight and how even with out a basic scheme there was still a solid structure to defense.

How do you view Danny's insight when it comes to strategy? Has detailed practice regiment and strategy been a contributing factor for you guys as a team?

Danny is the mad genius. He is solely our strategist he is the reason we have made these improvements. Overall a great leader.

Would you say Noah has stepped into the situation and taken it in stride, seemingly becoming the star player or the team overnight?

Noah seems to have been the missing puzzle piece. We all have a more support player background and it is nice to finally have someone who is more of that dedicated fragger role. He instantly clicked with the team which is solid too.

What adjustments need to be made to have a perfect season ending run and maybe make playoffs?

OUR ATTACK. I cannot stress enough Attack is our weakest thing in the game. At this point we just need 2 wins and we win. So now we need to find ways to so that.
